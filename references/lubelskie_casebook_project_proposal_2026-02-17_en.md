## 1) Core Diagnosis (official evidence summary)

1. Regional structure:
- Based on OECD (2025), Lubelskie is a low-development eastern EU border region; GDP per capita improved from about 35% of the EU average (2004) to 54% (2022), but remains low.
- On innovation, PCT patents per million population are far below EU/OECD averages (report notes 10x+ gap).

2. Labour-market vulnerability (registered unemployment, 2025-12, GUS):
- Lubelskie overall: `8.1%`
- High-unemployment counties: `Wlodawski 17.8%`, `Hrubieszowski 16.3%`, `Krasnostawski 14.0%`, `Chelmski 13.4%` (Tomaszowski `9.8%`)
- Comparison: `Lublin city 4.5%`

3. War-linked border pressure (2025, aggregated from 12 monthly NOSG PDFs):
- Total person crossings (Ukraine section): `7,316,228/year`
- Monthly range (persons): `433,965 ~ 842,594/month`
- Total truck crossings (Ukraine section): `818,322/year` (monthly `60,728 ~ 74,088`)
- Annual persons by road crossing: `Hrebenne 2,381,413`, `Dorohusk 1,567,185`, `Zosin 1,375,244`, `Dolhobyczow 1,190,131`
- Annual trucks by road crossing: `Dorohusk 420,310`, `Hrebenne 172,744`, `Dolhobyczow 119,067`, `Zosin 106,201`

Interpretation:
- Lubelskie simultaneously faces `low productivity / labour-population vulnerability` and `border logistics/human-flow volatility`.
- Therefore, a bundled package (`transport + energy + labour transition`) is more persuasive than a single-sector infrastructure project.

## 3) Concrete Lubelskie project proposal (recommended package)

Program name:
- `LUB-EAST Resilience & Transition Package (2026-2034)`

Design principles:
- Stage 1 (currently feasible): `2026-2027` self-help/pilot actions under the current system (ERDF/ESF+)
- Stage 2 (scenario): `2028-2034` expansion under a combined ERDF + conditional JTF + ESF+ structure

### 3.1 Project A: Border Mobility & Logistics Resilience

- Implementing entities:
  - Lead: `Wojewodztwo Lubelskie` (Marshal Office)
  - Partners: `Gmina Lublin`, `Chelm city`, `Powiat Chelmski`, `Powiat Hrubieszowski`, `Powiat Tomaszowski`, `PKP LHS`, transport operators
- Budget proposal:
  - Total `EUR 72m`
  - 2026-2027 pilot `EUR 18m` (ERDF 12 + local/national 6)
  - 2029-2034 scale-up `EUR 54m` (ERDF 36 + JTF 12* + other 6)
  - `*If JTF is not confirmed, replace with ERDF/national co-financing scenario`
- Project period:
  - Pilot: `2026-03-01 ~ 2027-12-31` (eligible spending may continue until `2029-12-31` under contract rules)
  - Scale-up: `2029-01-01 ~ 2034-12-31`
- Target area:
  - `Dorohusk-Chelm corridor`, `Hrubieszow-Zosin-Dolhobyczow corridor`, `Tomaszow Lubelski-Hrebenne corridor`, `Lublin functional area`
- Main actions:
  - Upgrade two transfer/interchange nodes (bus-rail-park/ride)
  - Deploy low-emission regional buses/feeder routes + digital integrated fare/operations management
  - Border-linked logistics ITS and congestion/air-quality mitigation
- Expected effects by 2034:
  - Shorter commuting/access times in border counties
  - Expanded low-emission public transport coverage
  - Better resilience to border-flow volatility

### 3.2 Project B: Border-County Clean Heat & Building Retrofit

- Implementing entities:
  - Lead: `WFOSiGW w Lublinie` + `Lubelskie Voivodeship`
  - Partners: high-unemployment county authorities, public hospitals/schools, housing management bodies
- Budget proposal:
  - Total `EUR 88m`
  - 2026-2027 pilot `EUR 11m` (ERDF 7 + local/national 4)
  - 2029-2034 scale-up `EUR 77m` (ERDF 44 + JTF 18* + other 15)
- Project period:
  - Pilot: `2026-03-01 ~ 2027-12-31`
  - Scale-up: `2029-01-01 ~ 2034-12-31`
- Target area:
  - `Wlodawski`, `Chelmski`, `Hrubieszowski`, `Krasnostawski`, `Tomaszowski` + public facilities in `Lublin city`
- Main actions:
  - Deep retrofit of public/social housing, heating and ventilation efficiency improvements
  - Heat-source transition (heat pumps/high-efficiency systems) + distributed solar integration
  - Energy counseling and affordability support for vulnerable households
- Expected effects by 2034:
  - Lower heating burden in high-unemployment/vulnerable areas
  - Lower energy bills and emissions in public facilities
  - Local job creation in retrofit/energy services

### 3.3 Project C: Just Skills & SME Diversification for Border Labour Markets

- Implementing entities:
  - Lead: `Powiat Labour Offices` (Chelm/Hrubieszow/Wlodawa/Tomaszow)
  - Partners: `Gmina Lublin/MUP`, regional universities (UMCS, Politechnika Lubelska, etc.), industry associations
- Budget proposal:
  - Total `EUR 26m`
  - 2026-2027 pilot `EUR 5m` (ESF+ 3 + ERDF 1 + local 1)
  - 2029-2034 scale-up `EUR 21m` (JTF 10* + ESF+ 7 + ERDF 4)
- Project period:
  - Pilot: `2026-06-01 ~ 2028-12-31`
  - Scale-up: `2029-01-01 ~ 2034-12-31`
- Target area:
  - `Wlodawski`, `Chelmski`, `Hrubieszowski`, `Tomaszowski`, `Lublin city` (advanced training hub)
- Main actions:
  - Reskilling for low-emission mobility maintenance, building energy, and logistics digital operations
  - Tailored job matching for vulnerable unemployed and transition-risk workers
  - SME transition micro-grants (process upgrade + energy efficiency)
- Expected effects by 2034:
  - Higher re-employment rates in vulnerable areas
  - Lower transition costs and higher productivity for SMEs
  - Social conflict mitigation during transition (income/employment channel)

### 3.4 Program summary (recommended)

- Total budget: `EUR 186m`
- By stage:
  - 2026-2027 self-help/pilot: `EUR 34m`
  - 2029-2034 scale-up: `EUR 152m`
- Funding mix (scenario):
  - ERDF-led + ESF+ workforce transition + conditional JTF + national/local co-financing

## 4) Integrating Russia-Ukraine war impacts (strongly recommended)

Conclusion:
- `Yes, this should be explicitly integrated.` Lubelskie is a border region, so feasibility, risk management, and schedule design are directly affected.

### 4.1 Short term (2026-2027)

- Signal:
  - High monthly variability in people/logistics crossings
- Design implication:
  - Front-load Project A (mobility/logistics) and run Project C (labour-market buffering) in parallel

### 4.2 Medium term (2028-2030)

- Signal:
  - Investment-risk premium may persist under prolonged conflict
  - Conversely, reconstruction-preparation demand can increase border logistics/service demand
- Design implication:
  - Submit `risk scenarios (conservative/base/expansion)` with 2028-2034 applications

### 4.3 Long term (2031-2034)

- Signal:
  - If reconstruction scales up, Lubelskie's forward-support hub potential rises
- Design implication:
  - Keep the combined package logic (transport/logistics + skills/transition) as the core

## 5) Ready-to-use slide wording (safe version)

1. Current-system limit:
- `Lubelskie is currently able to finance important ERDF/ESF+ actions, but this architecture alone cannot fully absorb the labour and social transition risks in high-vulnerability border counties.`

2. 2026-2027 self-help phase:
- `We therefore propose a 2026-2027 self-help and pilot phase focused on border mobility resilience, clean-heat retrofit, and labour-market transition measures.`

3. 2028-2034 expansion:
- `From the first feasible post-2027 calls, we scale to a multi-fund package (ERDF base, ESF+, and conditional JTF), with implementation planned for 2029-2034.`

4. Confirmed vs scenario split:
- `The project logic is fixed; exact JTF eligibility and final allocations remain subject to post-2027 legal adoption and territorial designation.`
